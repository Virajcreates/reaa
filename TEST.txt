Q1: What is the fee charged by K-RERA for correction of a project name?
A1: The fee charged by K-RERA for correction of a project name is Rs. 10,000/-.

Q2: What is the charge for third party transfer of a real estate project under K-RERA?
A2: The charge for third party transfer is 50% of the cost paid for registration of the project.

Q3: Under which section of the RERA Act is K-RERA empowered to fix standard fees for various services?
A3: Under Section 34(e) of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016.

Q4: What is the reference number of the circular on registration of real estate agents?
A4: The reference number is RERA/FINANCE/CR/100/2021-22, dated 30-03-2022.

Q5: What percentage of the amount collected from allottees must a promoter deposit in the RERA Bank Account?
A5: The promoter must deposit seventy percent (70%) of the amount collected/realised from allottees (other than pass through charges and indirect taxes) in the RERA Bank Account. However, if the estimated cost of construction and land cost is higher than estimated value of sales revenue, one hundred percent (100%) must be deposited.

Q6: What three certificates are required for withdrawal of funds from a RERA Bank Account?
A6: The three certificates required are: (1) Certificate from the Architect (Form 1), certifying the percentage of completion of construction work; (2) Certificate from the Engineer (Form 2), for the actual cost incurred on construction work; and (3) Certificate from a Chartered Accountant in practice (Form 3), for the cost incurred and paid on construction cost and land cost.

Q7: What is a "No Lien Account" as defined in the RERA Bank Account Directions?
A7: A No Lien Account is a bank account without any third party rights or security interests.

Q8: Can a promoter change the RERA Bank Account from one bank to another without permission?
A8: No, the promoter may change the RERA Bank Account from one bank to another only with prior permission of the Authority.

Q9: What was the deadline for registration of ongoing real estate projects under RERA in Karnataka?
A9: The deadline for registration of ongoing projects was 31 July 2017, as the Act came into force on 1 May 2017, and promoters had three months to register.

Q10: What penalty can a promoter face for contravening Section 3 of the RERA Act (failing to register a project)?
A10: Under Section 59(1), a promoter can face a penalty of up to ten percent of the estimated cost of the real estate project. If the promoter continues to violate, under Section 59(2), they can face imprisonment up to three years or a fine of up to a further ten percent of the estimated cost, or both.

Q11: What penalty can an unregistered real estate agent face under Section 62 of the RERA Act?
A11: An unregistered real estate agent is liable to a penalty of ten thousand rupees for every day during which such default continues, which may cumulatively extend up to five per cent of the cost of plot, apartment or buildings of the real estate project.

Q12: How many registration requests had been received through the RERA Karnataka portal by midnight 31 July 2017?
A12: Over 700 registration requests (700+) had been received through the RERA Karnataka portal by midnight 31 July 2017.

Q13: When was COVID-19 declared a pandemic by the World Health Organization?
A13: The WHO declared COVID-19 as a pandemic on March 11, 2020.

Q14: What extension was provided to KRERA registered projects due to COVID-19 as per the circular dated 04-04-2020?
A14: For all KRERA registered projects where completion date, revised completion date, or extended completion date expires on or after 15th March 2020, the period of validity for registration was extended by three months. The time limits of all statutory compliances due in March/April/May were extended to 30th June 2020.

Q15: What was the fee for conciliation under the CDR Cell of K-RERA?
A15: The fee for conciliation was Rupees Five Hundred (Rs. 500/-), payable by the first party upon receiving consent from the opposite party.

Q16: How many days does the opposite party have to convey consent for conciliation under the CDR Cell?
A16: The opposite party has to convey his/her consent for conciliation within seven days.

Q17: What happens if parties fail to reach an amicable settlement through the CDR Cell?
A17: If the parties fail to reach an amicable settlement, the conciliation process will stand terminated and the disputes will be pursued as per the Act before K-RERA Dispute Redressal Mechanism or before any other court or forum.

Q18: Under which section does K-RERA derive the power to facilitate amicable conciliation of disputes?
A18: Under Section 32(g) of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016.

Q19: What is the appeal fee for filing an appeal before the Karnataka Real Estate Appellate Tribunal (KREAT)?
A19: The appeal fee is Rs. 5,000/-.

Q20: What is the head of account for remitting the appeal fee and copying fee to KREAT?
A20: The head of account is 0216-02-800-0-01-000, and it shall be remitted by generating a challan at www.k2.karnataka.gov.in.

Q21: What is the copying fee charged per page by KREAT?
A21: The copying fee is Rs. 2/- per page.

Q22: How should the pre-deposit of 30% of the penalty be made when filing an appeal before KREAT?
A22: It should be made by Demand Draft or Banker's Cheque payable at Bengaluru in favour of "The Registrar, Karnataka Real Estate Appellate Tribunal, Bengaluru", along with a memo mentioning the respective parties' names and appeal number.

Q23: What is the GST rate applicable for all categories of houses with effect from 1.7.2017?
A23: The uniform rate of GST for all categories of houses is 12%, except under Affordable Housing Schemes where GST is charged at 8% (w.e.f. 25.1.2018).

Q24: Under which section of the CGST/SGST Acts must builders pass on the benefit of Input Tax Credit to buyers?
A24: Under Section 171 of the CGST/SGST Acts, 2017.

Q25: Where can buyers lodge complaints if builders do not pass on GST Input Tax Credit benefits?
A25: Buyers can complain to the State Screening Committee, Commissioner of State Tax, or Central GST, or to the National Anti-profiteering Authority (NAA) through their website www.naa.gov.in or email secretary.naa@gov.in.

Q26: What is the procedure for incorporating a modified sanction plan in a registered project?
A26: The promoter must take prior approval from K-RERA before incorporating the modified sanction plan. They must submit the modified plan approved by the competent authority along with the required documents through the K-RERA web portal for review and approval.

Q27: What guidelines must promoters follow when releasing advertisements in print and electronic media?
A27: Promoters must include the K-RERA registration number in all advertisements. They must not advertise unregistered projects or projects without valid registration. The advertisement must contain accurate information consistent with what is registered with K-RERA.

Q28: What is the reference number of the circular regarding quarterly updates for registered real estate projects?
A28: The reference number is K-RERA/299/QU/2024, dated 17/5/2024.

Q29: What documents must a promoter provide for quarterly updates to K-RERA?
A29: Promoters must submit updated project progress information, financial statements showing amounts collected and utilized, architect/engineer certificates on completion status, and bank account statements for the designated RERA bank account, all through the K-RERA web portal.

Q30: What is the fee for further extension under Section 7 of the RERA Act 2016?
A30: The fee for further extension was prescribed under circular RERA/Finance/CR-150/2022-23 dated 31/1/2023 issued by K-RERA.

Q31: What are the obligations of digital portals advertising real estate projects?
A31: Digital portals must: (1) maintain self-imposed discipline to not disseminate information about non-registered properties without K-RERA registration details; (2) register as real estate agents with K-RERA within two months if their activities function as a real estate agent within Karnataka; and (3) comply with advertisement guidelines issued on 14/11/2019. Portals that only advertise (without discharging agent functions) must include a disclaimer that they are simply advertising agencies.

Q32: Under which section of RERA does the definition of Promoter include land owners?
A32: Under Section 2(zk) of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016.

Q33: What obligations does a Landowner/Promoter have regarding the designated bank account?
A33: The Landowner/Promoter must ensure that 70% of the sale proceeds realised from the allottees of the landowner's share (in case of area sharing) are deposited to the designated account of the real estate project, in case the transactions are done before obtaining the Completion Certificate/Occupancy Certificate.

Q34: What is the penalty date for ongoing projects registering under RERA after the deadline?
A34: As per the official memorandum dated 01.12.2017, any applications for ongoing projects submitted after the deadline of 31.07.2017 will only be considered upon payment of a penalty. Applications submitted in November 2017 or later are treated as violations of Section 3 of the RERA Act, with a mandatory penalty of up to 10% of the total estimated project cost.

Q35: What extension was granted to real estate projects due to COVID-19 second wave, and till what date?
A35: K-RERA extended the time limit till 01-10-2021 for projects whose completion date expires on or after 01-04-2021, as per the notification dated 27-08-2021 (RERA/SIC/CR-04/2019-20), based on the Government Housing Department's direction.

Q36: What is the defect liability period under the RERA Agreement for Sale?
A36: Under the 2020 Agreement for Sale Rules, any structural defect or other defect in workmanship, quality, or provision of services brought to the notice of the Promoter within 5 (five) years from the date of handing over possession must be rectified without further charge within 30 (thirty) days. Under the 2018 Revised Agreement, the Promoter must rectify defects within 90 days.

Q37: Within how many days must a promoter refund money to an allottee upon cancellation under the 2020 Agreement for Sale?
A37: The promoter must return the balance amount (after deducting the booking amount) within 45 days of cancellation under the 2020 Agreement for Sale Rules.

Q38: Within how many days must a promoter refund money upon cancellation under the 2018 Revised Agreement of Sale?
A38: The promoter must return the balance amount within 60 days of cancellation under the 2018 Revised Agreement of Sale.

Q39: What is the Standard Operating Procedure for execution of awards/orders issued by K-RERA?
A39: As per circular RERA/PS/SOP-1/CR/2020-21 dated 28/10/2020, the SOP outlines the procedure for execution of awards and orders, including timelines for compliance, notice periods, and enforcement mechanisms for orders passed by the Authority and Adjudicating Officer.

Q40: What must a promoter do before transferring or assigning rights and liabilities to a third party?
A40: As per circular KRERA/circular/02/2019 dated 27/08/2019, the promoter must obtain prior written approval from K-RERA, submit the prescribed application along with supporting documents including the proposed agreement between existing and incoming promoter, consent from allottees, financial statements, and affidavits as specified in the prescribed Annexure-A format.

Q41: What guidelines exist for change of project name under K-RERA?
A41: As per circular dated 27.11.2019 (RERA/LS/CR-05/2019-20), the promoter must submit an application through K-RERA's web portal with supporting documents including an affidavit, consent documents, board resolution, and pay the prescribed fee of Rs. 10,000/- for the change.

Q42: What are the steps in the K-RERA project registration process?
A42: The process involves: Step I - Submit legible documents, declaration in Form B as prescribed, and complete all mandatory fields. Step II - Receive acknowledgement via email. Step III - Application scrutinized by Authorized Officer within 5 days. Step IV - Applicant must remove defects within 5 days of first communication, 3 days for second. Step V - If complied, registration certificate in Form C issued; if rejected, Form D issued. Step VI - If rejected, 30 days to rectify under Rule 6.

Q43: What is the consequence of not responding to defect communications during project registration?
A43: If the promoter fails to comply after two communications, the Authority shall issue a final notice calling upon the promoter to furnish documents within 7 days and to hear. If the promoter still fails to comply, the Authority will reject the application by forfeiting the fee paid by the promoter for non-compliance.

Q45: What extension measures were taken in the circular dated 19/05/2020 regarding COVID-19?
A45: The circular (K-RERA/Secy/04/2019-20 dated 19/05/2020) extended the registration and completion timelines for registered real estate projects. It also addressed the adjournment of complaint hearings before K-RERA and the Adjudicating Officer due to the lockdown conditions. Statutory compliance timelines were extended until 15-09-2020.

Q46: What further COVID extension was provided by the circular dated 18/12/2020?
A46: The circular (RERA/SEC/CR-04/2019-20 dated 18/12/2020) provided an additional three months extension for completion of real estate projects affected by COVID-19, building upon the earlier extensions granted in April and May 2020.

Q47: What is the annual audit requirement for promoters under the RERA Bank Account Directions?
A47: As per the third proviso of Section 4(2)(I)(D), the Promoter must get accounts audited within six months after the end of every financial year by the statutory auditor of the Promoter's enterprise and produce a report on statement of accounts on project fund utilization and withdrawal (Form 4). Importantly, the chartered accountant certifying Form 3 (for withdrawals) must be a different entity than the one certifying Form 4.

Q48: What is the procedure for filing a complaint through the K-RERA Conciliation and Dispute Resolution Cell?
A48: The party initiating conciliation files an online application which is automatically emailed to the opposite party. The opposite party must convey consent within 7 days. Upon consent, the first party pays Rs. 500 as fees. The matter is then referred to the appropriate CDR Cell, and parties are intimated of the date, time, and venue. If settlement is reached, a consent agreement is drawn and signed. The consensus agreement is binding on both parties.

Q49: How should the RERA Bank Account be named as per the Bank Account Directions?
A49: It shall be required to suffix the term "RERA Bank Account for <name of the project> Real Estate Project" with the name of the RERA Bank Account holder. Each project must have only one RERA Bank Account opened in the local branch where the project is going on.

Q50: What are the key differences between the 2020 Agreement for Sale and the 2018 Revised Agreement of Sale?
A50: Key differences include: (1) Refund timelines - the 2020 version prescribes 45 days for refunds while the 2018 version prescribes 60 days; (2) Defect rectification - the 2020 version requires 30 days while the 2018 version allows 90 days; (3) The 2018 version includes FAR (Floor Area Ratio) disclosure requirements for promoters in the Additional Constructions clause; (4) The 2018 version includes provisions for phased developments under Section 15.4; (5) The 2018 version explicitly addresses HUF-connected land scenarios under warranty clause (X); (6) The 2020 version specifically references the Karnataka Apartment Ownership Act, 1972 and the Karnataka Ownership Flats Act, 1972, while the 2018 version uses generic state law references.
